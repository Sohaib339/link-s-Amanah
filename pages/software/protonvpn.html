Introduction

ProtonVPN is a security-focused VPN service created by the team behind ProtonMail, the world's largest encrypted email service. Based in Switzerland, ProtonVPN benefits from some of the strongest privacy laws in the world and operates under Swiss jurisdiction.

Founded in 2017 by scientists who met at CERN, ProtonVPN was built with a commitment to transparency and security. All of its applications are open source and have been independently audited, allowing anyone to verify that the software does what it claims.

ProtonVPN is unique in offering a genuinely free tier with no data limits, no ads, and no logs. This makes secure VPN access available to everyone, regardless of ability to pay. Premium plans add more servers, faster speeds, and additional features.

Key Features

  • Free Tier Available: Unlimited data, no ads, and no logs on free plan
  • Swiss Privacy: Protected by strong Swiss privacy laws
  • Open Source: All apps are open source and independently audited
  • Secure Core: Route traffic through privacy-friendly countries
  • Kill Switch: Prevents data leaks if VPN connection drops
  • NetShield: Block malware, ads, and trackers (paid feature)

How to Use

1. Connecting with Free Tier

  1. Open ProtonVPN and sign in
  2. Free servers are marked and available in US, Netherlands, Japan
  3. Click on a free server location
  4. Wait for connection to establish
  5. Your traffic is now encrypted

2. Using Quick Connect

  1. Click the "Quick Connect" button
  2. ProtonVPN selects the fastest available server
  3. For free users, connects to best free server
  4. Premium users get access to faster servers

3. Enabling Kill Switch

  1. Go to Settings > Connection
  2. Enable "Kill Switch"
  3. Choose between Standard or Permanent mode
  4. Standard blocks traffic only when VPN was active
  5. Permanent always requires VPN for internet access

4. Using Secure Core (Paid)

  1. Enable Secure Core in settings
  2. Traffic routes through Switzerland, Iceland, or Sweden first
  3. Provides extra protection against network attacks
  4. Slightly slower but more secure

Common Problems & Fixes

Problem 1: Free servers are slow

  • Free servers have more users; try during off-peak hours
  • Try a different free server location
  • Consider upgrading for faster dedicated servers

Problem 2: Connection keeps dropping

  • Switch to a different VPN protocol (OpenVPN, WireGuard)
  • Check your internet connection stability
  • Try connecting to a different server
  • Disable battery saver modes

Problem 3: Cannot log in

  • Verify your Proton account credentials
  • Check if you're using ProtonMail credentials (same account)
  • Reset password if needed
  • Ensure internet connection without VPN

Problem 4: DNS leaks detected

  • Enable DNS leak protection in settings
  • Use ProtonVPN's DNS servers
  • Disable WebRTC in your browser
  • Check for conflicting VPN software

Conclusion

ProtonVPN stands out for its commitment to privacy, transparency, and accessibility. Its open-source apps, Swiss jurisdiction, and genuinely useful free tier make it an excellent choice for privacy-conscious users.

The free version provides essential VPN protection without compromises on security, while paid plans offer additional speed, servers, and features. Whether you're a casual user or a privacy advocate, ProtonVPN delivers trustworthy protection.
